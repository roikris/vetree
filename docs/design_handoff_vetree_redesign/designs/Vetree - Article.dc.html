Pimobendan in Preclinical Myxomatous Mitral Valve Disease

Boswood A, Häggström J, Gordon SG, Fox PR, Wess G, et al. · Journal of Veterinary Internal Medicine · 28 June 2026

Clinical bottom line

Pimobendan delayed the onset of congestive heart failure in preclinical MMVD dogs with cardiomegaly by a median of roughly 15 months — without an increase in adverse events.

Summary

This multi-centre, randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial enrolled dogs with preclinical myxomatous mitral valve disease (MMVD) and echocardiographic and radiographic evidence of cardiac enlargement. Dogs received either pimobendan or a matched placebo and were followed until the primary endpoint — onset of congestive heart failure or cardiac-related death.

Treatment with pimobendan significantly prolonged the preclinical period. The median time to the primary endpoint was extended by approximately 15 months relative to placebo, and the benefit was consistent across subgroups defined by age, breed and baseline heart size.

The safety profile was reassuring: adverse events were comparable between groups, supporting early initiation of therapy once cardiomegaly is confirmed rather than waiting for clinical signs to develop.
